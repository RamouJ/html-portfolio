Who am I

What's good! I can be described in 3 words more or less; Ambitious, Humorous, and Self-sufficient. I am a rising sophomore at the University of Washington, and intending to study Informatics. I have grown up in the Seattle Area almost all of my life, but my parents hail from Gambia, West Africa. I am also the middle child in my family with an older and younger sister, so my natural desire to stand out and diverge from the pack, is pretty much rooted in my upbringing. As well as my drive to succeed and reach my goals so I can give back to the communities that helped to shape me into the Queen I am today.

What do I do

I am a college student, so I do not have very much time outside of my studies to spend on the things I care about. However, when I am not in the classroom or the library, I like to spend time enjoying nature, exploring my surroundings, excercising, learning new programming languages, and creating websites using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, such as this one! I also love to get out in the community and work with young females and males of color, to get them thinking about STEM.

What are my goals

My ultimate goals in life, include opening institutions back home in Gambia, such as schools, clinics, and markets to help stimulte the economy, and overall, helping more black females to fill tech and computer science roles. Ever since I discovered computer science and how you can create anything with it, I have made it my life mission to show other low-income students of color, the power of technology and how they can use Computer Science to reach thier wildest dreams.
